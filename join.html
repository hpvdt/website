Join our team


HPVDT is a student organization dedicated to learning and teaching engineering principles through extraordinary, world-class projects. Our previous work includes the world’s first flapping-wing human powered aircraft, a human-powered submarine, and the fastest tandem bike in the world at 120 km/hr. We are pushing the limits of human power, sustainability, and efficiency in transportation.

You can learn more at hpvdt.skule.ca/wiki

Roles

We are looking for new team members to lead design and manufacturing efforts in our next big project. Although anyone can apply to become a team member, subsystem leadership roles will be offered to the most competitive candidates. In any role, you will be responsible for a part or subsystem with the continuous support of the rest of the team.

General Member

About you:
• You are excited about hands-on learning.
• You are comfortable dealing with challenges you’ve never encountered before.
• You can manage your time effectively between school and extracurricular activities.
• You are self-driven and not afraid to ask questions or to give/receive feedback.
>
What you can expect to do as part of our team:
• SolidWorks CAD.
• Woodworking, machining, and/or other manufacturing & prototyping.
• FEA and CAM.
• Applying fluid mechanics and/or aerodynamics.
• Experience in any or all of these are assets, but if you don’t have that experience, don’t worry! We’ll help you cultivate these skills. People in any year of school are welcome to apply.

Electronics Team Member

About you:
• You are excited about hands-on learning.
• You are comfortable dealing with challenges you’ve never encountered before.
• You can manage your time effectively between school and extracurricular activities.
• You are self-driven and not afraid to ask questions or to give/receive feedback.

What you can expect to do as a part of our Team:
• Programming in C/C++, which are our primary languages.
• Applying electrical engineering concepts like soldering and digital multimeter use.
• Work with Arduinos, Raspberry Pis or other microcontrollers.
• PCB design through KiCAD, which is our primary electronics design software.
• Experience in any or all of these are assets, but if you don’t have that experience, don’t worry! We’ll help you cultivate these skills. People in any year of school are welcome to apply.
